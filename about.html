Who I Am

Hello, my name is Cheyenne Butler-Howell, and I am an active-duty member of the United States Air Force pursuing my undergraduate degree while serving. Balancing military responsibilities with my college education has strengthened my discipline, adaptability, and ability to manage competing priorities. Although this path can be demanding, it has also reinforced my commitment to continuous learning and challenged me to grow both personally and professionally.

My Interest in Web Development

I became interested in web development because it allows me to combine creativity, structure, and problem-solving in a practical way. I enjoy taking a basic idea and developing it into a functional website through intentional design and organized code. Each assignment has helped me build confidence with HTML and CSS while also showing me the importance of accessibility, responsive design, and user experience in creating effective websites.

My Goals

My goal is to continue expanding my technical knowledge while strengthening my skills in web development and design. As I progress through my education, I hope to learn additional programming languages, deepen my understanding of front-end development, and create websites that are both visually engaging and accessible to a wide range of users. I am excited to continue growing as a developer and applying what I learn to future academic and professional opportunities.

Beyond the Classroom

Outside of school and work, I enjoy traveling with my husband and experiencing different cultures. Living overseas has given me opportunities to visit places I never imagined seeing and has helped me appreciate different perspectives and ideas. Those experiences have made me more adaptable and continue to inspire both my personal and professional growth.
